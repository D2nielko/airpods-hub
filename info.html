📡

Your AirPods are always talking

AirPods continuously broadcast Apple’s “proximity pairing” message over Bluetooth LE — unencrypted bytes carrying model, color, battery levels, charging state, in-ear status, and even a counter of how many times the case lid has been opened. That’s exactly what this app’s battery page listens for.

🔋

Battery is reported in 10% steps

The BLE broadcast encodes each battery as a 4-bit value from 0–10 (so 7 = 70%), with 15 meaning “not available.” The smooth-looking percentage on your iPhone is built from this same coarse data plus the richer encrypted channel Apple devices get.

🎚️

Adaptive EQ — the EQ you can’t touch

AirPods Pro tune low and mid frequencies in real time using the inward-facing microphone to measure what’s actually reaching your eardrum. There is no public API to control it — which is why third-party EQ (like this app’s) must shape the audio signal instead of the device.

🩺

An FDA-authorized hearing aid

With iOS 18, AirPods Pro 2 gained a clinical-grade Hearing Test, Hearing Protection, and an FDA-authorized over-the-counter Hearing Aid feature — the first mainstream earbuds cleared for that use.

👂

Skin-detect, not light-detect

AirPods Pro 2 replaced the older optical in-ear sensor with a dedicated skin-detect sensor so playback doesn’t pause when you put them in a pocket or on a table — it distinguishes skin from other surfaces.

🫧

The vent system

Each AirPod Pro has vents that equalize pressure between your inner ear and the outside — that’s why they don’t give you the “plugged” feeling of regular silicone earbuds during ANC.

📍

The case is a tracking device

The AirPods Pro 2 case carries its own U1 ultra-wideband chip and a built-in speaker, so Find My can guide you to it with direction-and-distance Precision Finding and make it chirp — even when the earbuds aren’t in it.

🔄

Firmware updates happen silently

There’s no “update” button for AirPods. Firmware installs automatically when they’re in the case, charging, and near a paired Apple device on Wi-Fi. Android users have no official way to trigger or even check for updates.

🤖

Android gets the basics only

AirPods pair with any Bluetooth device as standard headphones, but battery popups, ear detection, Spatial Audio, and seamless switching are all Apple-only. Projects like LibrePods and OpenPods reverse-engineered the protocols to bring some of it back.

🎛️

Left and right swap roles

One AirPod is always the “primary” that talks to your phone; the other relays from it. The BLE battery broadcast literally flips which nibble means “left” depending on which pod is primary — a detail every third-party battery reader has to handle (including this one).

🍏

The irony of this web app

This app runs on Android, macOS, Windows, and Linux via Web Bluetooth in Chrome/Edge — but not on iPhone, because Safari doesn’t implement Web Bluetooth. The one platform where AirPods are first-class is the one platform this app can’t reach them from.

⚡

Charging-case math

Six hours from the buds plus 24 more from the case = 30 hours total. A 5-minute emergency top-up buys about an hour — roughly one commute per 1% of case battery.
